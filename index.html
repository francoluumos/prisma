Frame

One piece of carbon

A carbon-fibre monocoque, laid up and cured as a single shell. No bonded junctions to add weight or flex — the whole frame works as one structure, shaped tube by tube in the wind tunnel.

Carbon fibre · 8.3 kg net

Build

Built without seams

A high-modulus T800 carbon cockpit, internal routing, and a 24-speed drivetrain on hydraulic disc brakes. Nothing breaks the airflow and nothing rattles — the cockpit reads as one clean line from saddle to axle.

24-speed · hydraulic disc

Ride

Holds a line

A rigid racing frame on 700c carbon wheels: light, stiff and planted at speed, the deep-section rims carrying momentum on the flat while the carbon tubes soak up the road.

700c carbon · 150 kg load
