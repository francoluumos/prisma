# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

A broad, general consumer audience — not design-literate specialists, but people
who recognize quality when they feel it. They arrive curious, on any device,
often from social or referral. Their context is short-attention browsing: the
page has seconds to earn a slower second look. The "job" is emotional before it
is rational — they want to feel that this thing is special before they care what
it costs or does.

## Product Purpose

Prisma is the launch landing page for the **Prisma One** — a precision aero road
bicycle: a single-piece carbon monocoque frame, shaped in the wind tunnel, built
around the idea of putting as little as possible between rider and road. The hero
is the object itself — the silver carbon frame and deep-section wheels, shot on a
clean studio surface. The page exists to announce and dramatize the bike, not to
spec-sheet it. Success is an audience that leaves impressed and remembers the
machine: a page that signals exceptional engineering and craft and makes a visitor
feel they encountered something rare. Reservations follow from awe, not feature
lists. ("Prisma" carries a spectrum of light — used as a restrained brand accent,
never literal rainbow.)

## Brand Personality

**Luminous, refined, precise.** Premium and quiet-confident. The voice is sparse
and assured — it states, it doesn't sell. Spectrum light is the signature, but
handled with restraint: dispersion as a material, not a gimmick. The feeling is
holding a flawlessly-machined glass object up to the light — clean, weighty,
optically alive. Awe through precision, never through noise.

## Anti-references

- **Corporate / stock.** No corporate-blue palettes, no stock photography, no
  safe-and-forgettable enterprise styling. Nothing that reads as a template a
  thousand companies could share.
- Generic SaaS scaffolding (hero-metric blocks, identical card grids, eyebrow
  kickers on every section) is off-limits — this is a brand surface, not a SaaS page.
- Cheesy literal rainbows, gradient text, and decorative glassmorphism-everywhere:
  the spectrum must feel optical and earned, never novelty-toy.

## Design Principles

1. **The object is the hero.** Every layout decision serves making the device
   feel real, weighty, and optically extraordinary. The page frames it; it does
   not compete with it.
2. **Dispersion as material, not decoration.** Spectrum color comes from light
   passing through glass — physically motivated, disciplined, used where it earns
   its place. Never rainbow-for-rainbow's-sake.
3. **Restraint is the premium signal.** Generous negative space, few elements,
   each deliberate. Confidence is shown by what's left out.
4. **Awe through precision.** The wow comes from technical craft executed flawlessly
   — sharp type, exact alignment, smooth motion — not from volume or clutter.
5. **Earn the second look.** The first frame stops the scroll; the detail rewards
   the visitor who stays. Quality compounds the longer you look.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG 2.1 AA.** Body text ≥4.5:1 against its background; large/display text ≥3:1.
  On dark surfaces, verify spectrum-tinted text and placeholders hit the same bar —
  no light-gray-on-near-black that fails contrast for "elegance."
- **Reduced motion is required.** Every animation (dispersion shimmer, parallax,
  reveals) ships a `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` alternative — typically a
  crossfade or a static, already-beautiful default. Content is never gated behind
  a motion-triggered reveal.
- Spectrum/color effects must never be the sole carrier of meaning (color-blind
  safe): pair any color-coded information with text, shape, or position.
